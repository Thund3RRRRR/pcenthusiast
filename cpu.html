CPU (Central Processing Unit)

Think of the CPU as the brain of your computer. It holds the instructions for your computer, and lets it know what to do and when to do it. The more powerful the CPU, the more efficiently the computer will run. In the world of CPUs, there are 2 brands that you want to consider, each of which provide great processors that shine in their own categories.


The first of the two brands is AMD. AMD is primarily known for its capabilities in the media industry. These processors usually have many cores, allowing the computer to run multiple tasks like video editors, or anything to do with content creation at a very efficient speed.


If you are a gamer, and you are a building a computer targeted towards the performance of your computer while playing a game, then Intel's high instructions-per-clock (IPC) speed is the way to go for you.

As you may have noticed, Intel processors tend to be a little more expensive. But you do get what you're paying for, with the quality being comparatively better when contrasting the AMD and Intel series levels (I5 to Ryzen 5, I7 to Ryzen 7, etc). Ryzen processors are the more affordable option, and you get the most bang for your buck. However, in most cases, there is usually a need for an upgrade a little later down the line.

Cooling

If you don't really care about visual appearance, and you have an AMD CPU, you're in luck, becuase you barely have to worry about cooling. Most AMD CPUs come with their own stock cooler which is designed to nicely regulate the temperature of your CPU. Keep in mind that as the generations progress, AMD could refrain from putting coolers in their CPU packages, in which case you can order a stock cooler for anywhere between 20 and 80 dollars, depending on how colorful you want it to be. One more thing to make sure of when buying a cooler is that it's compatable with your CPU and motherboard, because not all of them are, while others require manual adjustment to fit the correct type.

If you do care about appearance, and you have the budget to put a nice touch of lighting into your PC, then you may want to consider water cooling. This method is significantly more effective when cooling your components, looks nice, and allows for temperature adjustment, but that doesn't mean that it's important. Standard fans and a stock cooler will get the job done just fine. Some things to consider when purchasing a water cooler (often called an AIO) is not only the price, but the installation process, which is almost always more complicated than the usual stock cooler.

RAM

The RAM sticks have a similar function to the CPU, in the sense that they boost the efficiency of the computer. But the bigger focus is that they allow for more applications to be running at once. Has your laptop ever been slowing down because you have too many tabs open? Or maybe you're downloading something while trying to play a game? The RAM is what is keeping track of running all of those things at once. Now typically, the average computer user is hardly ever using any more than 16 gigabytes of ram at a time. And even that is a huge stretch. You can easily get away with 8 gigabytes for just normal day to day usage, such as for work, or a game.

How Much Should I Get?

Gigabytes Practicality
8GB This is a perfect amount if you intend on using your computer for simple day to day activities. Just make sure to not have too many big applications open at once, like multiple of twitch streams, or recording yourself playing a game.
16GB This tends to be what most people settle on because of its lost lasting abilities. Almost always, no one needs more than 16GB of RAM. If you wanted to record yourself playing a game, and have 2 monitors going at once, 16 gigabytes will do the job just fine. This is the most common package you will see, and doesn't hurt to invest in.
32GB Yes, there is such thing as 24 gigabytes, but at that point it's better to just get 32. There are only a few specific types of people who will actually benefit from having this many gigabytes. People who stream their games, record them, have a computer powering 3 or more monitors at a time, and are simultaniously running multiple other programs are going to need this much RAM. Anything upwards of this amount will 1, require a special motherboard, and 2, not be relavent to you because those who need it will know it.

FAQ

Q A
What does MHz mean? MHz, or megahertz, is measuring exactly how fast each stick of ram is moving. The difference between this and having many gigabytes of space is that the gigabytes are what allow the quantity of data to be transferred between parts, rather than how fast that process actually happens. The range of MHz to try to stick around would be 3000-3600, which usually comes with a typical set of sticks.
How much do RAM sticks cost? The most common stick to find will have 8gb on it. And more commonly, they will be sold in sets of 2, because 16gb is the most common amount of RAM to be found in a PC. Each stick costs around $40, but this varies depending on if the RAM has RGB, or the amount of MHz each one has. But usually, you can find a perfectly good set of 16gb running at 3000-3600 MHz for about $75, which just so happens to be the sticks the computer that is being used to make this website has.
How will I know if my motherboard will support my sticks? This hardly ever is a problem, seeing as all sticks are designed to fit inside all motherboards. If there are any notable compatability issues, the description of the sticks would say so.

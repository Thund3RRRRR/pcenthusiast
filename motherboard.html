The Motherboard

What Is It?

If your computer was a town, the motherboard would be the town plaza. What this means, every part of the computer is eventually going to have something connecting back to the motherboard. This allows for communication between the components, and the ability to get power where it needs to be at all times. There are a few things to consider when deciding what motherboard to get. If you are trying to make a smaller and less expensive computer, buy a motherboard that will fit with your case. It's also very important to note that not all motherboards are compatable with all other brands of other components. You should be choosing your components after you choose your CPU and motherboard, because it will be a lot more tedious to be swapping out parts because some dont fit on the motherboard, whereas this problem wont occur if you already know what your motherboard does and doesn't support. Often times, the description of the product will contain all of the information regarding compatability. But you can always double check with google or pcpartpicker.com.
